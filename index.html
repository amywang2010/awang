About

I'm a Grade 10 student in Richmond Hill, Ontario. I started researching Alzheimer's disease at age nine, after my grandmother was diagnosed — and have been working on it since.

My primary project, "Window to the Mind," is a two-part computational pipeline for early Alzheimer's intervention. Part one is a ResNet-101 CNN (PyTorch) trained on retinal images that outperforms published OCT clinical benchmarks on an independent test set. Part two is RetinAD-1, a computationally designed prodrug candidate targeting beta-amyloid aggregation at the KLVFF motif, validated through AutoDock Vina, GROMACS molecular dynamics, and full ADMET profiling. The project earned Gold Medals at the York Region Science and Technology Fair and the Canada-Wide Science Fair, and placed me among 20 candidates for Team Canada at ISEF 2027.

In 2024, I founded Heartware, an intergenerational volunteer program now operating across five care homes, with 500+ participants and over $8,000 in accumulated funding. I also co-author clinical research with physicians at Xiangyang No. 1 People's Hospital, and direct logistics for Girls in Med's 80-competitor research symposium at UofT.

Across HOSA, DECA, and FBLC, I train and coach national-level competitors — two of my trainees placed 1st provincially at the HOSA Spring Leadership Conference.

Projects

01

Window to the Mind

A two-part pipeline for early Alzheimer's detection and drug intervention. Part one: a ResNet-101 (PyTorch) trained on the ODIR-5K retinal dataset — 91.8% accuracy, peak AUC-ROC 0.9971, 5-fold cross-validation, Grad-CAM validated. Grad-CAM heatmapping confirmed 545% greater model attention on the optic disc in Alzheimer's images, consistent with established clinical retinal nerve fiber layer findings. Part two: RetinAD-1, a myricetin-based prodrug screened from 500 structural modifications, validated via AutoDock Vina (−8.76 kcal/mol, PDB: 2BEG), GROMACS 10 ns MD simulation, MM-PBSA (ΔGbind = −15.80 kcal/mol), SwissADME, pkCSM, and RDKit. Passed all Lipinski, Ghose, Veber, and Egan drug-likeness filters. 340% higher predicted brain exposure than direct dosing.
